You have chosen to present Project B: "Investment in a machine for the production of regular fries". In the meeting you have to do a lot of persuasion. But at the end of the day, it is clear that your project will be carried out despite the initial resistance of your colleagues.

Now, 4 years after the start of the project and a lot effort from your side, you monitor its perfomance.
  • Remember: The project was designed to have a life span of 7 years with now 3 years remaining. You were convinced of the project from the start. The investment was worth $1 million and you estimated the annual cashflow at $270,000.
  • The expectations have been exceeded and the project has generated $330,000 annually for the first four years. However, doubts about the future performance of the project are now emerging. There is debate on whether the project should be continued or abandoned as annual cash flows are expected to decline. You estimate the performance for two different scenarios. There is a 25 % chance for a positive scenario which means the net present value would be $185,000. On the other side, there is a 75% chance that the net present value would be $135,000. the project's current salvage value is $177,500.
  • Especially because of your constant willingness to go the extra mile, you have a high reputation as a project manager. When it comes to project decisions, your colleagues appreciate your consulting skills. In the following meeting next week it should be decided whether the project should be continued or abandoned.
