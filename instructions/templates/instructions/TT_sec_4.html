Let’s sum up what we have learnt today:
  • Intuitive errors of thought happen continuously and are difficult to prevent.
  • Biases cannot always be avoided because System 2 may not be aware of the underlying error.
    • A prerequisite for reducing biases is increased monitoring and laborious activation of System 2. In everyday life, however, this is impractical and simply impossible. Since System 2 is simply far too slow and inefficient to act as a replacement for System 1 in routine decisions.
    A compromise would be desirable. Learning to recognize situations, where mistakes are likely to occur, and consequently to increase effort, to avoid far-reaching mistakes when the stakes are high, are crucial.
    In general, you should consider the following recommendations when it comes to important decisions:

  • Avoid getting caught in a trap: Question your intuition!
  • Try to overcome the laziness of your System 2 and take the mental effort, it requires to activate it.
  • Try to consider all relevant information of the situation.
  • And last but not least, think about alternatives and why they might occur or might be superior.
