Whether at work, in a restaurant or at sports, each of us makes hundreds of decisions every day and most of them intuitively. Only a small number of us take into consideration that we could be biased in our decision-making by environmental factors or even by ourselves. Conversely, the majority mistakenly assumes that their thinking is completely rational and uninfluenced. However, this presumption can have significant consequences in the form of so-called cognitive biases.
Within the next few minutes, I would like to address this very issue. Therefore, I would like to explain the causes and show you examples of situations in which cognitive biases can occur. This training will increase your awareness of cognitive biases and will in turn improve your decision making.
